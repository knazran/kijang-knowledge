Regulatory capital requirement seek to ensure that risk exposures of a financial institution are backed by an adequate amount of high quality capital which absorbs losses on a going concern basis. The capital adequacy framework sets out the approach for computing regulatory capital adequacy ratios, as well as the levels of those ratios at which a financial institution is required to operate. Learn about the conceptual design of capital requirement and the key policy features of Basel I, II and III in this programme.

This programme will be conducted with:

  • Corporate Governance Practices in the Bank.
  • BIS Core Principles
  • Surveillance


Objectives / Key benefits

  • Explain the BIS minimum regulatory capital and the various types of capital components; and
  • Assess the risk-weighted assets (RWA) and risk-weighted capital adequacy (RWCA)
